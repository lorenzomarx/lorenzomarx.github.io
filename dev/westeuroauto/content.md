## Design direction notes for `/frontend-design-pro`

- **Brand cue:** Alfa Romeo red is the natural accent — the hero image and logo image both feature a red Alfa. Pair with a confident dark neutral (charcoal/near-black) and warm off-white. Avoid generic "auto-shop blue".
- **Voice:** Owner-operated, proud, slightly old-school. Direct sentences. Don't over-polish — Nick's voice should still come through.
- **Typography:** A confident serif or strong geometric sans for headlines (think craftsmanship), workhorse sans for body.
- **Hero treatment:** Hero image has Nick + red Giulia in front of the roller door — let it carry the section. Headline can sit over a tinted gradient.
- **Quirks to keep:** The Foreman pigeon. The 38-year experience stat. "Behind McDonald's." These are the brand.
- **Quirks to clean up:** The original site has a typo "Disgnostic display" — fix to "Diagnostic display". Don't repeat.
- **Forms:** Form does not need a backend in v1 — `mailto:` submit or a stub that's clearly marked "TODO: wire up form handler" is fine.
- **Responsive:** Mobile is the priority — local NZ traffic, mostly searching from phones. Tap-to-call must be obvious.
- **Accessibility:** Strong contrast on red, alt text on every image (the inventory above is your alt-text source).
